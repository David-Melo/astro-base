MASTER RULE SET: READABILITY + SEO

Non‑negotiables

Write for humans first, search second.

Grade 7–9 readability.

Target term usage under 2 percent. Use variants and stems.

No em dash. No long horizontal dividers.

Every page is unique and valuable.

Purpose, Reader, Outcome
Goal: make the right reader say “this solves my problem.”

Before writing: define reader, pain, outcome, objections.

First 100 words must state who it is for, what it solves, why now, expected ROI or value.

Acceptance: a skim reader can summarize the page in one sentence after 10 seconds.

Quick checks

One core job to be done is explicit.

One primary CTA appears above the fold and matches intent.

Industry Depth (40 percent minimum)
Show you understand the market, not just the keyword.

Cover business model mechanics, buyer behavior, unit economics, demand cycles, ops bottlenecks, regulation, and competitive dynamics.

Include real examples, brief case snapshots, and time‑to‑value ranges when possible.

Acceptance: at least 40 percent of total words are industry‑specific substance.

Do

Use high‑intent phrases, long‑tail modifiers, and semantic families.
Avoid

Generic tips that fit any industry.

Structure For Humans and Crawlers
Make it obvious and scannable.

One H1 only. Use H2s for major sections. Use H3s only when needed.

Paragraphs under 4 lines. Use lists and tables where they clarify.

Add a concise “answer box” under the H1 when intent is answer‑seeking.

Acceptance: the H2s alone tell the story arc.

Formatting rules

Line length target 50–75 characters.

Strong verbs, short sentences, active voice.

Semantic HTML for lists, tables, quotes, and figures.

SEO + SXO + AEO + LLM Optimization
Search engines reward satisfaction. So do people.

Keep main keyword density under 2 percent; use proximity, stems, and natural variants.

Entities: name products, standards, locations, organizations, dates to anchor meaning.

SXO: fast load, mobile‑first layout, clear hierarchy, sticky or repeated CTA, low‑friction forms.

AEO: answer the primary query early, then deepen with steps, examples, and context.

Acceptance: the intro answers the likely question in under 60 words.

Technical on‑page targets

Title tag 50–60 characters with the primary term near the front.

Meta description 140–160 characters with a clear benefit.

Short, descriptive, hyphenated URL slug.

Descriptive alt text. Nouns and verbs, not keyword piles.

Add relevant schema: FAQ, HowTo, Product, Organization, LocalBusiness, or others that fit.

Brand and Tone Consistency
Voice: modern, sharp, commercial, confident.

Use the exact brand form per style guide. If multiple approved variants exist, apply them consistently.

Conversion mindset: benefits, proof, contrast, ethical urgency, clear next step.

Acceptance: each section moves the reader toward a decision.

Style guardrails

Oxford comma: yes.

Numerals for 10 and above. Percentages as numerals.

No clichés or hype without proof.

Length and Coverage
Right size for the job.

Pillars: 2,500–4,000 words for industry service pages. Flagship resources can exceed 4,000.

Complex verticals can lean high end.

Micro topics can be shorter but must fully resolve the query.

Acceptance: no “to be continued” feeling.

Intent Matching and Funnel Alignment
Meet readers where they are.

Identify intent: informational, navigational, commercial, transactional.

Map CTAs to stage: learn more, compare, get pricing, book consultation, start trial.

Acceptance: language and CTA match the reader’s stage.

Authority, Expertise, Trust (E‑E‑A‑T)
Prove it.

Show experience with specifics: data, names, timelines, constraints.

Cite credible sources when useful. Add short case snapshots and quotes.

Use consistent bylines, bios, and organization schema as needed.

Acceptance: a skeptical buyer finds enough proof to continue.

Performance and User Signals
Design for engagement and speed.

Clear information hierarchy, high contrast, legible fonts.

Aim for fast first paint and minimal layout shift.

Track scroll depth, time on page, and CTA clicks. Review monthly or quarterly.

Acceptance: above‑the‑fold communicates value and reduces pogo sticking.

Link Strategy and Content Ecosystem
Win topics with clusters.

Internal links support topic clusters and discovery of related content.

Link out to credible sources where it adds clarity or trust.

Avoid cannibalization by mapping one primary keyword per page.

Build authority through thought leadership and partnerships.

Acceptance: each page has a clear slot in the cluster with logical next reads.

No Duplicate, No Thin Content
Original or it does not ship.

Do not swap city or industry names over a template.

Use canonicals when overlap is unavoidable. Keep boilerplate minimal.

Acceptance: passes a uniqueness check and covers the topic fully.

Final Pre‑Publish Checks
Ship clean.

First 100 words state who, what, why, and value or ROI.

Main term density under 2 percent with natural variants.

Validate title, meta, slug, schema.

Internal and external links placed with intent.

CTA clear and aligned. Mobile readability tested.

Plan a quarterly refresh.

WRITER’S TOOLKIT

A) Content Brief Template

Audience: role, seniority, triggers, objections.

Job to be done: one sentence.

Intent: info, nav, commercial, transactional.

Promise statement: value in one line.

Primary keyword and 3–6 secondary terms plus stems and synonyms.

Industry depth targets: list 5 angles to cover.

Proof: data, quotes, case studies, numbers.

CTA: primary and secondary.

Cluster map: parent pillar, siblings, children.

Visuals: diagrams, tables, screenshots, with alt text notes.

B) Standard H1/H2 Skeleton For Industry Service Pages
H1: [SERVICE] for [INDUSTRY] in [LOCATION] that Improves [Outcome]

Short problem snapshot and an answer‑first summary

H2: Who We Serve and Why It Matters
H2: How [COMPANY] Solves [INDUSTRY] Pain Points
H2: What Makes This Market Hard
H2: Our Approach Step by Step
H2: Proof and Outcomes
H2: Pricing and Time‑to‑Value
H2: Implementation and Change Management
H2: Security, Compliance, and Risk
H2: FAQs
H2: Next Steps

C) Paragraph Micro‑Rules

Lead with the claim. Follow with proof or example.

Keep average sentence length under 20 words.

Use parallel structure in lists.

Replace vague modifiers with numbers or ranges.

D) Keyword Strategy Rules

Map one primary term per page. Assign 3–6 secondary and long‑tail variations.

Use proximity naturally. Place related terms near each other when they belong together.

Use stemming and semantic family terms to widen coverage without stuffing.

E) Visual and Alt Text Rules

Each visual must clarify a point or show evidence.

Filenames should describe the content, not just the keyword.

Alt text: what it is, what it shows, why it matters. Keep it concise.

F) Accessibility and Readability

Aim for grade 7–9. Short words and clear verbs.

High contrast, large tap targets, descriptive link text.

Respect heading order. Avoid “click here.”

G) Quality Gate Scorecard (100 points)

Purpose clarity and intro value: 10

Industry depth and specificity: 20

Structure and scannability: 10

Readability and tone fit: 10

SEO signals and schema: 10

AEO and entity richness: 10

Proof and E‑E‑A‑T: 10

SXO and CTA clarity: 10

Internal ecosystem fit: 5

Originality and non‑duplication: 5

Red Flags That Block Publishing

Keyword stuffing or density above 2 percent.

Advice that could fit any industry.

Undefined CTA or mismatched intent.

No proof elements.

Heavy jargon or tangled sentences.

FAQ Construction Rules

One FAQ section per page with 10–15 distinct questions.

Answers are 40–60 words, snippet‑ready, and industry‑specific.

Avoid duplication with other FAQs on the site.

Editorial Mechanics

No em dash.

Oxford comma: yes.

Single spaces after periods.

Numerals for 10 and above. Spell out one to nine unless a measurement.

Publishing Workflow

Strategy brief approved.

Outline with H1/H2 map approved.

Draft passes the 40 percent industry‑depth test.

SEO and schema pass.

Proof check: names, data, examples verified.

Mobile and speed spot checks.

Final QA using the scorecard.
